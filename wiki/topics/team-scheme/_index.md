# Team Scheme

Team-level context that determines player opportunity and value.

| Page | Description | Confidence |
| ---- | ------------ | ---------- |
| [proe](proe.md) | Actual pass rate minus a model's expected pass rate given game situation — isolates scheme-driven passing tendency from game-script variance; scramble classification is the main provider disagreement. | medium |
| [neutral-game-script-pass-rate](neutral-game-script-pass-rate.md) | Pass rate filtered to close-score situations, removing garbage-time inflation and clock-killing depression; the score-margin/time-window threshold is contested and unstandardized. | medium |
| [pace-of-play](pace-of-play.md) | Plays per game as a volume multiplier on all opportunity metrics; raw play count is confounded by efficiency, opponent pace, and game script — distinct from tempo (seconds per snap). | medium |
| [offensive-line-continuity](offensive-line-continuity.md) | Stability of the same five starters across games; sources genuinely disagree on how much independent predictive power it carries once team quality/selection bias is controlled for — treated as an open dispute, not settled. | medium |
| [offensive-line-blocking-efficiency](offensive-line-blocking-efficiency.md) | Pass-block and run-block efficiency metrics (charting/grading, timing-based win-rate, and outcome-adjusted families); confounded by QB pocket presence and RB vision respectively — no universal composite exists. | medium |
| [play-action-rate](play-action-rate.md) | Share of pass plays with a run fake; reliably boosts QB efficiency by freezing second-level defenders, but RPO classification is the dominant unresolved cross-provider disagreement — usage and efficiency must be evaluated separately. | medium |
| [rpo-usage-rate](rpo-usage-rate.md) | Rate of plays giving the QB a post-snap run/pass read; must be decomposed into pass/handoff/keep branches since the same team rate produces opposite fantasy effects depending on branch selection — no standardized cross-provider definition exists. | medium |
| [personnel-grouping-tendencies](personnel-grouping-tendencies.md) | 11/12/21 package frequency sets a structural floor on positional opportunity but never guarantees it; fullback/hybrid classification is the dominant cross-provider disagreement, and motion after the huddle can make the recorded package label diverge from actual on-field structure. | medium |
| [motion-usage-rate](motion-usage-rate.md) | Share of snaps with pre-snap player movement; the shift-vs-motion definitional boundary is the single largest cross-provider disagreement, and the motion-to-efficiency correlation is confounded by the talent/scheme that already produces it. | medium |
| [red-zone-efficiency-team](red-zone-efficiency-team.md) | Team-level red-zone entry rate vs. conversion rate; entry volume is stable and predictive, conversion (touchdown) rate is small-sample and regression-heavy, and QB rushing structurally suppresses RB goal-line opportunity independent of RB talent. | medium |
| [third-down-conversion-rate-team](third-down-conversion-rate-team.md) | Team third-down conversion rate is a downstream outcome of early-down efficiency and distance distribution, not an independent quality signal; distance-bucketed and neutral-script filtering are required before treating it as predictive. | high |
| [oc-play-calling-tendencies](oc-play-calling-tendencies.md) | OC tendency profiles (PROE, personnel usage, target distribution, tempo) are portable across teams only after confirming who actually calls plays and adjusting for personnel fit; PROE and pace are the most portable dimensions, target distribution the least. | high |
| [head-coach-aggressiveness](head-coach-aggressiveness.md) | Head coach fourth-down and two-point aggressiveness, measured as decision quality vs. a win-probability-optimal baseline rather than raw go-rate; modestly raises TD opportunity and play volume for efficient offenses while suppressing kicker value. | high |
| [offensive-scheme-identity](offensive-scheme-identity.md) | Coach-lineage scheme labels (Shanahan, Air Raid, West Coast, Coryell, spread-option) are low-weight priors on positional opportunity; observed functional tendencies (PROE, personnel, motion, play-action) and quarterback fit override the label once in-season data accumulates. | high |
| [target-distribution-concentration](target-distribution-concentration.md) | Target share and concentration indices (HHI, top-1/top-2 share) determine WR1/TE1 reliability, but must be conditioned on route participation, team pass volume, game script, and red-zone-specific distribution to avoid false-concentration and injury-distortion errors. | high |
| [backfield-committee-structure](backfield-committee-structure.md) | Backfield roles (early-down, passing-down, goal-line, three-down) must be decomposed by route/carry/target/goal-line share, not snap share alone; pass-protection trust gates passing-down role and injury redistribution is rarely proportional to one backup. | high |
| [defense-vs-position](defense-vs-position.md) | Raw DvP conflates opponent quality, touchdown variance, and game script with true defensive skill; requires opponent-adjustment and role/alignment-specific splits (slot vs. perimeter, RB rushing vs. receiving) to be actionable, and should rank below role, implied total, and EPA/DVOA in a decision hierarchy. | high |
| [opponent-defense-efficiency-epa-dvoa](opponent-defense-efficiency-epa-dvoa.md) | EPA (points-based, play-state) and DVOA (opponent-adjusted value-over-average) are distinct, complementary metrics that both outperform raw yards/points allowed; must be paired with success rate and explosive-play rate, and sack/scramble/RPO classification varies materially by provider. | high |
| [vegas-implied-team-total](vegas-implied-team-total.md) | The single strongest external predictor of weekly fantasy scoring opportunity; must be allocated through player-specific role and volume rather than applied directly, with blowout risk, stale lines, weather, divisional matchups, early-season lines, and short weeks materially degrading its reliability. | high |
| [weather-impact](weather-impact.md) | Wind is the dominant, most actionable weather variable (~15mph threshold, non-linear, disproportionate deep-ball impact); precipitation acts mainly through drops/fumbles, cold alone is a weak standalone factor once wind is controlled for, and domes neutralize all three. | high |
| [home-away-travel-splits](home-away-travel-splits.md) | Home-field advantage is real but modest (roughly 1.5-3 points) and travel effects are asymmetric — eastward travel is more disruptive than westward due to circadian displacement — but small-sample noise and opponent/game-script confounding make raw home/away splits unreliable as standalone signals. | medium |
