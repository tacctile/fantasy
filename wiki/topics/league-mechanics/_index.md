# League Mechanics

Scoring formats, draft strategy, and league structure.

| Page | Description | Confidence |
| ---- | ------------ | ---------- |
| [ppr-half-ppr-standard-scoring](ppr-half-ppr-standard-scoring.md) | Reception scoring restructures relative positional value through a replacement-level shift, not raw point addition; value must be measured as points-above-replacement, and target share/route participation predict PPR value better than yards-per-touch or touchdown rate. | high |
| [te-premium-scoring](te-premium-scoring.md) | TE Premium is a scarcity amplifier that benefits route-running, target-earning tight ends specifically — not the position broadly — and widens rather than closes the gap between the elite tight end tier and replacement-level options. | high |
| [superflex-two-qb-value-shift](superflex-two-qb-value-shift.md) | Superflex/2QB formats collapse the quarterback replacement-level baseline nonlinearly due to a hard ~32-starter NFL supply cap; 2QB (mandatory) is stricter than Superflex (optional), and rushing production is disproportionately valuable under common scoring. | high |
| [passing-td-value-4pt-6pt](passing-td-value-4pt-6pt.md) | The 4-to-6-point passing TD shift adds 2 points per TD uniformly, but the strategic effect is replacement-adjusted value gain for elite/efficient passers, not raw point inflation; rushing production remains disproportionately valuable, and the effect compounds heavily under Superflex/2QB. | high |
| [roster-construction-starting-lineups](roster-construction-starting-lineups.md) | Starting lineup requirements, bench size, and IR slots jointly determine positional demand and waiver liquidity; replacement-level baselines must be recalculated per league's exact roster shape, not borrowed from generic rankings. | high |
| [flex-spot-configuration](flex-spot-configuration.md) | Flex spots reallocate positional demand toward whichever eligible position wins the marginal comparison — RB/WR dominate standard flex, TE rarely competes without a premium, and QB-eligible (Superflex) flex functions as a de facto second QB slot. | high |
| [league-size-scarcity-effects](league-size-scarcity-effects.md) | League size sets replacement level by scaling starter demand against a non-linear NFL talent pool; RB and TE are most scarce-sensitive to team-count growth, single-QB is least sensitive, and superflex/2QB is a structural (not incremental) scarcity regime change for QB. | high |
| [dynasty-redraft-keeper-frameworks](dynasty-redraft-keeper-frameworks.md) | Redraft, dynasty, and keeper value players over fundamentally different time horizons — keeper is a distinct hybrid governed by retention cost, not "short dynasty"; dynasty age curves peak earliest and decline sharpest for RB, later and more gradually for WR, longest for QB/TE. | high |
| [best-ball-strategy](best-ball-strategy.md) | Best ball's auto-set lineup rewards ceiling and spike-week probability over floor, makes positive correlation (stacking) valuable via portfolio covariance, and favors Zero-RB/Hero-RB constructions; over-stacking and bye-week clustering are the most consistent failure patterns. | high |
| [average-draft-position](average-draft-position.md) | ADP is a market-price statistic, not a valuation — it is only meaningful relative to the exact population that generated it (league size, scoring, best ball vs. redraft, platform, time window), and conflating market price with player value is a leading source of draft error. | high |
| [adp-ecr-differential](adp-ecr-differential.md) | The ADP-ECR gap measures market-vs-expert disagreement, not correctness; it is only actionable once format-compatibility, tier structure, and gap persistence are confirmed, and evidence on its predictive value beyond draft-day negotiating signal is mixed. | medium |
| [value-based-drafting](value-based-drafting.md) | VBD's arithmetic (projection minus baseline) is trivial; nearly all its difficulty and failure modes trace to baseline selection — flex spots require pooling baselines across eligible positions, static pre-draft baselines decay and must be recalculated live, and superflex/best-ball require format-specific re-derivation rather than incremental adjustment. | high |
| [zero-rb-hero-rb-robust-rb](zero-rb-hero-rb-robust-rb.md) | Zero-RB, Hero-RB, and Robust-RB concentrate different risk types (timing, single-anchor, opportunity cost) rather than being ranked strategies; the running back "dead zone" (roughly rounds 3-5) is the most consistently corroborated strategy-independent trap, and format/lineup structure determines which philosophy is favored. | high |
| [late-round-qb-strategy](late-round-qb-strategy.md) | Late-round QB exploits replacement-level compression at quarterback in single-QB leagues; rushing production is the strongest differentiator of late-round hits, six-point passing-TD scoring narrows the strategy's edge, and it is structurally invalid in superflex/2QB formats. | high |
| [qb-stacking-strategy](qb-stacking-strategy.md) | Stacking a QB with his pass-catchers raises ceiling through positive score covariance without changing either player's expected points — near-mandatory in best ball/DFS tournaments, a secondary tiebreaker in season-long redraft, and prone to failure when forced beyond genuine correlation value. | high |
| [handcuff-strategy](handcuff-strategy.md) | Handcuff value is conditional, not standalone — the biggest failure pattern is mistaking depth-chart position for true workload succession, and league depth cuts opposite to intuition: handcuffing is generally more defensible in deep, thin-waiver leagues than in shallow ones. | high |
| [auction-draft-budget-allocation](auction-draft-budget-allocation.md) | Auction value converts to dollars via discretionary budget (total minus minimum-bid floor); stars-and-scrubs vs. balanced allocation are both conditional strategies, and the biggest failure mode is leaving budget unspent or overpaying fragile mid-tier players (the auction analog of the RB dead zone). | high |
| [rookie-draft-capital-landing-spot](rookie-draft-capital-landing-spot.md) | Draft capital is the strongest single rookie evaluation signal because it encodes organizational commitment and job security; landing spot is a real modifier, not a substitute, and format determines relative weighting — dynasty favors capital/talent, redraft favors immediate opportunity. | high |
| [breakout-candidate-modeling](breakout-candidate-modeling.md) | Opportunity signals (route participation, target share, snap share) are far more predictive and stable than efficiency signals for identifying breakouts; the strongest bets require both an expanding role and a market price that still reflects the old role. | high |
| [bust-risk-regression-modeling](bust-risk-regression-modeling.md) | Touchdown-rate overperformance is the strongest bust-risk signal, but elite players and strong offenses must regress toward their own baseline, not a generic league-average rate, to avoid systematic false positives. | high |
| [waiver-wire-faab-strategy](waiver-wire-faab-strategy.md) | FAAB should be front-loaded early (roughly 40-50% by week 4) since unspent late-season budget has zero terminal value, and bids should be built on confirmed role certainty rather than recent box-score production. | high |
| [trade-value-calculation](trade-value-calculation.md) | Trade value is value-over-league-specific-replacement, not a generic chart lookup; the two-for-one consolidation trap (summing chart values overstates split-package gains) is the most consistently corroborated trade-evaluation failure pattern. | high |
| [bye-week-management](bye-week-management.md) | Bye cost is the gap between a starter and the best realistic replacement, not the player's full point total; every source agrees talent should never be sacrificed at the draft to avoid a bye overlap. | high |
| [playoff-schedule-strength](playoff-schedule-strength.md) | Weeks 15-17 schedule strength is strictly a late tiebreaker between similarly valued players, never a primary signal; raw fantasy-points-allowed is a flawed proxy prone to small-sample noise and defensive drift since preseason. | high |
