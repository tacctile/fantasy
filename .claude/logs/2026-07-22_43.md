## Build — items 1–2 (tiers.ts) → [x][x]

`src/services/bpa/tiers.ts` `computePositionTiers(players)` — pure, groups base-value ranked pool by position, filters to `projectedPoints >= replacementPoints` (down-to-replacement scope, per-player operative line — never re-derived), sorts projection desc / id asc, computes consecutive gaps, break where `gap > TIER_GAP_MULTIPLE[pos] × baselineGap`. `summarizeTiers(board)` → per-position `{ tierCount, topTierSize, topTierTopProjection }` for item 4's counter.

**Baseline statistic — MEDIAN, not mean (live-verified robustness decision, disclosed):** started with mean ("average gap", wiki-literal) but the live 2026 pool showed QB/TE collapsing to 2 tiers (target 3–5) — a single dominant elite-separation gap (QB 40.7, TE 31.5 vs mean ~6–9) inflates the mean enough to swallow the position's secondary cliffs. Switched baseline to the median consecutive gap (robust to the one outlier) with a mean fallback when median is 0 (dense K/DEF zero-gap tails). This is a within-Clarify latitude call (Nick chose "avg/median") disclosed at decision time.

**Multiples (item 2 — declared-silence platform constants, live-tuned to the target bands):** RB 3.0 / WR 3.2 / QB 2.0 / TE 1.5 / K,DEF 2.0 / default 2.5. Deep positions (RB/WR) carry the HIGHER multiple — their dense near-replacement tail has a tiny median gap, so a higher multiple stops the tail fragmenting into noise tiers — while the resulting ABSOLUTE point threshold (multiple × tiny median) stays SMALLER than QB/TE's, which IS the build-file's "tighter for RB/WR" sensitivity (documented; the multiple looking "inverted" vs the wording is the median baseline's scale effect, same intent). Target counts are a sanity check only.
